Component Risk Assesment Dashboard

This dashboard helps evaluate component-level supply risk across lead-time volatility, supplier availability, geographic concentration, criticality, and cost exposure. It can be used to identify which components need monitoring, dual-sourcing, safety stock planning, or deeper redesign discussion, while also giving teams a clear summary view for supplier reviews and sourcing decisions.

How to Use This Dashboard

This dashboard helps evaluate component-level supply chain risk and sourcing readiness by combining disruption scoring, supplier inputs, cost exposure, and historical shortage intelligence. Use it to compare components, stress-test assumptions, prioritize dual-sourcing, and explain sourcing decisions to cross-functional stakeholders.

What the dashboard is built for

Primary use: identify which components need monitoring, dual-source qualification, safety stock, or strategic redesign discussion.

Typical users: global supply managers, sourcing teams, operations leaders, manufacturing program teams, and stakeholder reviewers.

Best use case: when you want a single place to connect supplier readiness, disruption exposure, and recommendation logic.

Recommended workflow

1. Start with Summary to see the decision snapshot.

2. Move to Component Risk Scorecard to inspect detailed risk drivers.

3. Use Scoring Framework to test assumptions and scenario changes.

4. Check Supplier Data and Historical Supply Disruptions for sourcing context.

Scoring Framework

Use the formulas at the top for quick reference, adjust each standalone scoring box in the middle, and review the live preview at the bottom before applying changes to the dashboard.

Quick Formula — Composite Risk

Composite Risk = (Lead × 0.30) + (Capacity × 0.30) + (Geo × 0.40)

Priority Score = Composite Risk × Criticality

Quick Formula — Cost Exposure

Cost Exposure = (BOM Score × 0.40) + (Price Volatility Score × 0.35) + (Single-Source Exposure × 0.25)

Quick Formula — Recommendation Logic

Monitor: < 8 | Dual-source: 8–14.9 | Safety stock: 15–19.9 | Strategic: ≥ 20

These thresholds use Priority Score, not Composite Risk alone.

Composite Risk weights

Composite Risk = (Lead × Lead Weight) + (Capacity × Capacity Weight) + (Geo × Geo Weight)
Weights used to calculate Composite Risk from Lead, Capacity, and Geo scores.

Default values are shown in brackets.

BOM Score levels

BOM percentage thresholds used to convert cost share into a 1–5 BOM score.

Cost Exposure weights

Cost Exposure = (BOM Score × BOM Weight) + (Price Volatility Score × Price Weight) + (Single-Source Exposure × Single-Source Weight)
Weights used to calculate Cost Exposure from BOM, price volatility, and single-source exposure.

Default values are shown in brackets.

Criticality mapping

Priority Score = Composite Risk × Criticality
Numeric criticality values used to convert Low, Medium, and High criticality labels into Priority Score.

Glossary

Plain-language definitions for every score used in the dashboard, plus how each one is calculated.

Lead Score

What it means: How unstable lead time becomes under stress.

How it is calculated: Peak Lead Time ÷ Normal Lead Time, then converted into a 1–5 score using the editable thresholds.

Capacity Score

What it means: How much flexibility exists in the supplier market.

How it is calculated: Uses the number of viable suppliers and converts that count into a 1–5 score.

Geo Score

What it means: How exposed the component is to geographic concentration and geopolitical risk.

How it is calculated: Uses the existing geo basis in the dataset and maps it into a 1–5 score through editable thresholds.

Composite Risk

What it means: Overall supply disruption risk.

How it is calculated: Weighted blend of Lead Score, Capacity Score, and Geo Score.

Criticality

What it means: How important the component is to keeping the product or process functional.

How it is used: Multiplies Composite Risk to create Priority Score.

Priority Score

What it means: The urgency of supply risk after importance is considered.

How it is calculated: Composite Risk × Criticality.

BOM %

What it means: Approximate cost share of the component inside the total product bill of materials.

How it is used: Converted to BOM Score using the editable BOM % thresholds.

BOM Score

What it means: Cost significance of the component within the product.

How it is calculated: Converts BOM % into a 1–5 score.

Price Volatility Score

What it means: How much the component’s market price tends to move over time.

Why it matters: Higher price volatility creates margin risk, budgeting difficulty, and negotiation pressure.

How it is used: Combined with BOM Score and Single-Source Exposure to compute Cost Exposure.

Single-Source Exposure

What it means: How dependent the component is on a limited supplier base.

How it is calculated: Uses supplier count to estimate exposure, with fewer suppliers creating higher scores.

Cost Exposure

What it means: Overall financial sensitivity if the component becomes more expensive or supply tightens.

How it is calculated: Weighted blend of BOM Score, Price Volatility Score, and Single-Source Exposure.

Recommendation

What it means: Suggested sourcing action.

How it is calculated: Uses the Priority Score thresholds to classify each component as Monitor, Dual-source candidate, Safety stock required, or Strategic priority / redesign consideration.
