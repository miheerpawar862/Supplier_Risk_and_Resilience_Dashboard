What the scores mean

Composite Risk Score

Overall supply disruption risk. It blends Lead-Time, Capacity, and Geographic scores into one 1–5 score.

Priority Score

Production importance once criticality is applied. Formula: Composite Risk × Criticality.

Recommendation

Action guidance generated from the Priority Score thresholds in the Scoring Framework tab.

The Scoring Framework tab now lets users edit not only the weights and thresholds, but also the actual score levels used for Lead-Time Volatility, Capacity Responsiveness, and Geographic Concentration Risk.

Scoring logic with editable default levels

Users can edit the score levels for each pillar, the formula weights for Composite Risk, the criticality mapping, and the decision thresholds for Recommendation.

Lead-Time Volatility Score

Lead-Time Volatility Ratio = Peak Lead Time ÷ Normal Lead Time

Default levels
Score 5 applies when the ratio is greater than or equal to the final threshold.

Capacity Responsiveness Score

Capacity Score = function(number of viable suppliers)

Default levels
Score 5 applies when viable suppliers are less than or equal to 1.

Geographic Concentration Risk Score

Geo Score = function(country concentration + geopolitical exposure)

Default levels
Score 5 applies when geo average is above the final threshold.

Composite Risk formula weights

Composite Risk = (Lead-Time × 0.30) + (Capacity × 0.30) + (Geo × 0.40)

Keep the three weights totaling 1.00 for clean interpretation.

Priority Score formula and criticality mapping

Priority Score = Composite Risk × Criticality

Recommendation thresholds

Monitor: < 8 | Dual-source: 8–14.9 | Safety stock: 15–19.9 | Strategic: ≥ 20

Level 0 — Raw Inputs

Criticality, Normal Lead Time, Peak Lead Time, viable supplier count, and geo exposure are the base inputs.

Level 1 — Pillar Scores

Users can now edit the actual level bands for Lead-Time Volatility, Capacity Responsiveness, and Geographic Concentration Risk.

Level 2 — Composite Risk Score

Composite Risk combines the three pillar scores using editable weights.

Level 3 — Priority Score

Priority Score applies criticality so high-importance parts rise faster in ranking.

Level 4 — Recommendation

Recommendation turns Priority Score into an action: Monitor, Dual-source, Safety stock, or Strategic priority.
